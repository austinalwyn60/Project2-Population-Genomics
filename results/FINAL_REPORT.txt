INTRODUCTION
================================================================================
Pharmacogenomics is the study of how genetic variations influence an individual's response to drugs. 
Current pharmacogenomic guidelines for drug dosing (especially for genes like CYP2C19, CYP2D6, 
SLCO1B1, and VKORC1) are predominantly based on European population data. 

India, with a population of over 1.4 billion and high genetic diversity, is significantly 
underrepresented in these studies. This creates a serious clinical risk where standard dosing 
recommendations may lead to treatment failure or adverse drug reactions in Indian patients.

This project performs a focused comparison of 10 clinically actionable pharmacogenomic variants 
across South Asian (SAS), European (EUR), African (AFR), and East Asian (EAS) populations using 
public 1000 Genomes Phase 3 data.

================================================================================
OBJECTIVE
================================================================================
• Identify 10 important pharmacogenomic variants from PharmGKB
• Compare allele frequencies across 4 populations
• Highlight differences between South Asian and European populations
• Discuss clinical implications for Indian patients

================================================================================
METHODS
================================================================================
• Data Source: 1000 Genomes Phase 3 VCF files
• Populations: SAS, EUR, AFR, EAS
• Tools: R with ggplot2 package for visualization
• 10 variants selected based on clinical importance

CLINICAL INTERPRETATION
================================================================================

1. rs4244285 & rs12248560 (CYP2C19)  
   Frequency is more than 2 times higher in South Asians. This variant reduces the activation 
   of Clopidogrel (anti-platelet drug). Indian patients may have higher risk of treatment failure 
   after heart procedures. May require higher dose or alternative drugs like Prasugrel/Ticagrelor.

2. rs4149056 (SLCO1B1)  
   18% in SAS vs 8% in EUR. This variant increases risk of statin-induced muscle toxicity 
   (myopathy). Indian patients on statins (Atorvastatin, Simvastatin) need careful monitoring 
   or lower starting dose.

3. rs8099917 (IFNL3)  
   Higher frequency in South Asians. Associated with response to interferon-based therapy 
   (used in Hepatitis C). May influence treatment success rates.

4. rs3892097 (CYP2D6)  
   Higher in SAS. Affects metabolism of many antidepressants, antipsychotics, and painkillers 
   (e.g., Codeine, Tamoxifen). Risk of toxicity or poor efficacy.

5. rs9923231 (VKORC1)  
   High frequency in all populations but slightly higher in SAS. Major gene affecting Warfarin 
   (blood thinner) dosing. Indians may require different maintenance doses.

6. rs1057910 & rs1799853 (CYP2C9)  
   Affect metabolism of Warfarin and several NSAIDs. Combined with VKORC1, they are critical 
   for safe anticoagulation therapy.

7. rs1045642 (ABCB1)  
   Affects drug transport. Can influence absorption and effectiveness of many drugs.

8. rs1142345 (TPMT)  
   Affects Thiopurine drugs (used in leukemia and autoimmune diseases). Low frequency but 
   important to test before starting treatment.

================================================================================
CONCLUSION
================================================================================
This project clearly demonstrates that South Asian populations have significantly different 
allele frequencies compared to European populations for several key pharmacogenomic variants. 

European-based dosing guidelines may not be appropriate for Indian patients and could lead to 
treatment failure or adverse effects. There is an urgent need to develop India-specific 
pharmacogenomic guidelines and databases.
